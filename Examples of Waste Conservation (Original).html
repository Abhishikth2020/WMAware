Examples of action to improve Waste Conservation

Example 1: India
In October of 2019, the Prime Minister of India, Narendra Modi, intended to ban single use plastics by 2022; however, the effectiveness of the ban is questioned with it’s possibility to disrupt the food, consumer goods, and plastic production industries. Jobs were also at risk in plastic production factories. According to views from news articles, to ensure a sustainable model, targeting people more, instead of manufacturing companies, to take responsibility to dispose plastic properly and to reduce the usage of plastic is a more effective solution. When less people buy and use plastic goods, manufacturing companies lose profits and are pressured to find environmentally friendly alternatives to plasic for restoring their profits).


Example 2: China
Shezen, a city in China, initiated a waste to energy facility in response to a predicted a population of 12 million people in 2025. The facility, manufactured by Schmidt Hammer Lassen Architects and Gottlieb Paludan Architects, intends to burn 5000 metric tonnes of waste per day. This would serve as an eco-friendly model for other cities to follow and is sustainable since the large amounts of waste used by people can be used in a productive and beneficial manner. However, the release of greenhouse gases from burning waste is a disadvantage which can cause air polluion. Additionally, treatment of gases before disposal is also important.


Example 3: Brazil
With Rio de Janeiro, a city in Brazil, hosting the Summer Olympics Game in 2016, combating pollution and treating waste were important for creating a clean city for the games. On the other hand, there were also many instances of people recklessly throwing trash in inappropriate areas. The games on August 5th, 2016 used barriers for waste collection with GPS systems to map water currents with waste. These measures were beneficial to control the amount of waste polluted; however, multiple clean up operations would be needed (increasing costs and affecting economy) for clearing plastic completely and regulating citizen behavior is needed.
